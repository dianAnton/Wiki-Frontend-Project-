# Marcus Aurelius

Marcus Aurelius (121â€“180 AD) was a Roman Emperor and Stoic philosopher, born in Rome. He ruled from 161 to 180 AD as the last of the "Five Good Emperors." Marcus is best known for his philosophical writings, particularly the "Meditations," a series of personal reflections and philosophical musings. These writings reveal his Stoic beliefs, emphasizing virtue, self-discipline, and acceptance of fate. Despite the challenges of his time, including wars and the Antonine Plague, Marcus Aurelius is remembered for his commitment to duty and ethical leadership.
